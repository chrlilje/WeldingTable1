Rotary Encoder: Recommended Direction for Your Project Goals

Given your goal is maximum readability for beginners, the cleanest path is:

Start with interrupt on A channel only.
In ISR, read B channel to determine direction.
Keep ISR minimal: only increment/decrement a volatile counter.
In main loop, copy and clear delta atomically, then apply logic there.
Add simple debounce/noise guard with minimum pulse interval if needed.
Why this is a good fit:

Conceptually easy to explain.
Good performance with low ISR complexity.
Keeps most logic in normal loop code where students are comfortable.
If later you need higher precision/noise robustness, move to a small state-table decoder (00/01/11/10 transitions), but that is less beginner-friendly initially.
